**第二章：互联网、万维网与电子商务**

#### **1. Introduction to the Internet and the Web (互联网与Web简介)**

*   **The Internet (互联网)**
    *   **Definition:** An interconnected mesh（网络） of computers and data lines, connecting millions of people and organizations. It is a large global network that connects together smaller networks all over the globe.
        *   **中文解释:** 互联网是计算机和数据线的互联网络，连接着全球数以百万计的人和组织。它是一个连接全世界小型网络的巨大全球网络。
    *   **Origin (起源):** Launched in 1969 as a US-funded project called **ARPANET** (Advanced Research Project Agency Network).
        *   **中文解释:** 互联网起源于1969年，是一个名为ARPANET（高级研究计划局网络）的美国资助项目。
    *   **Physical Nature (物理构成):** It is a physical network made up of **wires, cables, satellites, and rules** (protocols（协议）).
        *   **中文解释:** 互联网是一个实体网络，由电线、电缆、卫星和规则（协议）组成。
    *   **Protocol (协议):** Based on the **TCP/IP** protocol suite.
        *   **中文解释:** 互联网建立在TCP/IP协议的基础上。

*   **The World Wide Web (万维网)**
    *   **Definition:** The Web provides an easy-to-use, multimedia interface to resources available on the Internet.
        *   **中文解释:** Web（万维网）为互联网上的可用资源提供了一个易于使用的多媒体界面。
    *   **Origin (起源):** Introduced in 1991 at **CERN** by Tim Berners-Lee.
        *   **中文解释:** 1991年由蒂姆·伯纳斯·李在欧洲核子研究中心（CERN）推出。
    *   **Relationship with the Internet (与互联网的关系):**
        *   The Web is **a part of the Internet**, it is **NOT** the Internet itself.
        *   The Internet is the physical network; the Web is the interface to access information on that network.
        *   **关键点:** Web是互联网的一部分，而不是互联网本身。互联网是物理网络，Web是访问网络资源的界面。
    *   互联网 (Internet) = 基础设施 (Infrastructure)
    *   万维网 (Web) = 网站等东西 (Websites and related things)
    *   QQ、微信、大多数网游客户端不属于万维网


*   **Evolution of the Web (Web的演变)**
    *   **Web 1.0:** Linking existing information. (链接现有信息)
    *   **Web 2.0:** More dynamic content creation and social interaction. (更动态的内容创建和社交互动)
    *   **Web 3.0:** Identifies relationships between data. (识别数据之间的关系)
    *   **Web 4.0:** Focuses on connecting data and devices into a seamless integration into your physical life. (专注于将数据和设备无缝集成到你的物理生活中)

*   **Common Internet Uses (常见的互联网用途)**
    *   Communicating (通讯), Shopping (购物), Searching (搜索), Education or e-learning (教育或在线学习), Online Entertainment (在线娱乐).

#### **2. Accessing the Web (访问Web)**

*   **Internet Service Provider (ISP) - 互联网服务提供商**
    *   **Function:** The most common way to access the Internet is through an ISP. They provide a connection for individuals using telephone lines, cable, and wireless connections.
        *   **中文解释:** ISP是接入互联网最常见的方式，它们为个人用户提供连接服务。
    *   **Connection Technologies:** DSL, Cable, Wireless.

*   **Browsers (浏览器)**
    *   **Definition:** Programs that provide access to web resources and allow you to explore the web.
        *   **中文解释:** 浏览器是提供网页资源访问并允许你浏览网页的程序。
    *   **Popular Browsers:** Apple Safari, Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox.
    *   **URL (Uniform Resource Locator) - 统一资源定位符**
        *   **Definition:** The address or location of a resource that must be specified in the browser.
        *   **Structure (结构):** Has at least two parts:
            1.  **Protocol (协议):** Rules for exchanging data (e.g., `http`).
            2.  **Domain Name (域名):** The specific address where the resource is located (e.g., `www.google.com`).
        *   **Top-Level Domain (TLD) - 顶级域名:** Identifies the type of organization.
            *   `.com` - Commercial (商业)
            *   `.edu` - Educational (教育)
            *   `.gov` - Government (政府)
            *   `.mil` - US Military (美国军事)
            *   `.net` - Network (网络)
            *   `.org` - Organization (组织)

*   **HTML and Hyperlinks (超文本标记语言和超链接)**
    *   **HTML (Hypertext Markup Language):** The language used to create web pages. Browsers interpret HTML commands to display the content.
        *   **中文解释:** HTML是创建网页的语言，浏览器通过解释其命令来显示页面。
    *   **Hyperlinks (links):** Connect to other web pages, text files, graphic images, audio, and video clips.
        *   **中文解释:** 超链接（或链接）用于连接到其他网络资源。

*   **Interactive Web Technologies (交互式Web技术)**
    *   **JavaScript:** A language executed on the **user's computer** to trigger interactive features (e.g., opening new windows, checking online forms).
        *   **执行位置:** 用户端（客户端）。
    *   **PHP:** A language executed on the **website's computer** (server) to improve a website's interactivity.
        *   **执行位置:** 网站端（服务器端）。
    *   **Cascading Style Sheets (CSS):** Separate files that control the appearance of web pages to ensure a consistent look.
        *   **中文解释:** CSS用于控制网页的外观和风格，保持页面风格的一致性。

#### **3. Web Utilities (Web实用工具)**

*   **Definition:** Specialized utility programs that make using the Internet and the web easier and safer.
*   **Filters (过滤器)**
    *   **Function:** Block access to selected sites and can monitor internet usage. Often used for parental control.
        *   **中文解释:** 阻止对特定网站的访问，并监控网络使用情况。
*   **File Transfer Utilities (文件传输工具)**
    *   **Function:** Allow for transferring files (downloading and uploading) across the Internet.
    *   **Types:**
        1.  **Web-based file transfer services:** Use a web browser to transfer files, no custom software needed.
        2.  **BitTorrent:** Distributes file transfers across many different computers. Well suited for very large files.
        3.  **FTP (File Transfer Protocol) / SFTP (Secure FTP):** Efficiently copy files to and from your computer. Often used for uploading changes to a website.
*   **Internet Security Suites (互联网安全套装)**
    *   **Definition:** A collection of utility programs designed to maintain your security and privacy online.
    *   **Functions:** Protect against computer viruses, provide filters, etc.
    *   **Cost:** Typically less than purchasing each function separately.
    *   **Examples:** Norton Internet Security, Bitdefender Internet Security.

#### **4. Internet Communications (互联网通信)**

*   **Communication is the most popular Internet activity.**
*   **Social Networking (社交网络)**
    *   **Definition:** Connecting people and organizations that share a common interest or activity.
    *   **Common Features:** Profiles, Pages, Groups, Friends, News feed, Share settings.
*   **Blogs and Microblogs (博客和微博)**
    *   **Blogs:** Personal websites, time-stamped, arranged with the newest item first (e.g., WordPress).
    *   **Microblogs:** Designed for mobile devices and limit the size of posts (e.g., Twitter).
*   **Podcasts and Wikis (播客和维基)**
    *   **Podcasts:** Audio programs delivered over the Internet. Must be downloaded and saved to a computer to be run. (iPod + broadcasting)
    *   **Wikis:** A website that allows visitors to use their browser to add, edit, or delete the site's contents. It supports collaborative writing (e.g., Wikipedia).
*   **Messaging (消息)**
    *   Includes: Text Messaging, MMS, Instant Messaging.
*   **E-mail (电子邮件)**
    *   **Definition:** The transmission of an electronic message over the Internet. Can contain graphics, photos, and file attachments.
    *   **Basic Elements:**
        *   **Header (邮件头):** Address, Subject, Attachments.
        *   **Message (正文):** The letter or note.
        *   **Signature (签名):** Additional information about the sender.
    *   **Types of E-mail Systems:**
        *   **Client-based:** Requires an e-mail client program installed on your computer (e.g., Microsoft Outlook).
        *   **Web-based (Webmail):** Does not require a special program; accessed through a web browser (e.g., Gmail).
*   **Spam (垃圾邮件)**
    *   **Definition:** Unwelcome e-mail.
    *   **Tools to control spam:** Spam blockers/filters.
    *   **CAN-SPAM Act:** A law that sets the rules for commercial email (antispam laws).

#### **5. Search Tools and Content Evaluation (搜索工具与内容评估)**

*   **Search Tools (搜索工具)**
    *   **Function:** Help users locate needed information on the web.
    *   **Spiders:** Special programs used by search tools to continually look for new information and update databases.
*   **Search Engines (搜索引擎)**
    *   **Definition:** Specialized programs that help locate information based on **keywords** or **phrases**.
    *   **Hits:** The list of related sites returned by a search engine.
    *   **Examples:** Google, Bing, Duckduckgo.
*   **Content Evaluation (内容评估)**
    *   **Key Idea:** Not all information found on the web is accurate.
    *   **Evaluation Criteria (评估标准):**
        *   **Authority (权威性):** Is the author an expert?
        *   **Accuracy (准确性):** Has the information been critically reviewed?
        *   **Objectivity (客观性):** Does the author have a personal bias?
        *   **Currency (时效性):** Is the information up to date?

#### **6. Electronic Commerce (电子商务)**

*   **E-commerce (电子商务):** The buying and selling of goods over the Internet.
*   **Three Basic Types:**
    1.  **B2C (Business-to-Consumer):** Sale of products and services to the general public. This is the fastest-growing type.
        *   **Examples:** Online Banking, Online shopping (e-retailing). Amazon.com is a B2C site.
    2.  **C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer):** Individuals selling to each other.
        *   **Example:** Web auctions.
    3.  **B2B (Business-to-Business):** Sale of a product or service from one business to another.
*   **Security in E-commerce (电子商务安全)**
    *   **Challenge:** Developing fast, secure, and reliable payment methods.
    *   **Two Basic Payment Options:**
        *   **Credit card (信用卡)**
        *   **Digital cash (数字现金)**

---

#### **7. Cloud Computing (云计算)**

*   **Definition:** Uses the Internet and the web to shift computing activities from users' desktops to other computers on the Internet.
    *   **中文解释:** 将计算活动从用户桌面转移到互联网上的其他计算机。
*   **Benefit:** Frees end-users from owning, maintaining, and storing software programs and data.
*   **Three Basic Components (三个基本组成部分):**
    1.  **Clients (end-users):** Corporations and end users who want access to data, programs, and storage anywhere and anytime.
    2.  **Service providers:** Organizations that provide access to software, data, and storage over the Internet (e.g., Google Apps).
    3.  **The Internet:** Provides the connection between clients and providers. The **speed and reliability** of this connection are critical factors.

---

#### **8. Internet of Things (IoT) (物联网)**

*   **Definition:** The continuing development of the Internet that allows everyday objects embedded with electronic devices to send and receive data.
    *   **中文解释:** 互联网的持续发展，允许嵌入了电子设备的日常物品发送和接收数据。
*   **Examples:** Smartphones, wearable devices (可穿戴设备).
*   **Technology:** Uses **Web 3.0** applications.
